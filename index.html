05   Key Insight
The size-of-prize principle

The most important moment in the session. Braeden identified it himself: failing to quantify the prize for casual labour had held the team back from challenging timelines and being more demanding about progress. Without a defined prize, the organisation lacks the urgency to accelerate when results warrant it and the clarity to deprioritise when they don't.

This is not a project management insight — it is a leadership one. It applies across everything the organisation is building.

Standing discipline going forward: For every initiative entering the pipeline, the team defines the prize — quantified where possible — before any trial begins. This single habit, consistently applied, addresses the front-end weakness and creates natural velocity when results are strong.

 
06   Development Objectives
Three priorities for the next phase
01
Innovation Fluency
Develop the ability to articulate what excellent innovation practice looks like with the same confidence Braeden brings to operational excellence. Not comprehensive knowledge — fluency. The ability to name it, recognise it, and develop others toward it.
Progress indicator
Can describe 3–4 characteristics of excellent innovation with examples. Begins developing others using this language.
02
Delegation by Trust
Shift from delegation by framing (solving then handing over) to delegation by trust (handing over the problem). Build others' ownership rather than their alignment with Braeden's prior framing.
Progress indicator
Regular reflection on 'what did I drive this week that someone else could have owned?' Evidence of team initiating and resolving without prior framing.
03
Culture Architecture
Translate the cascade vision into a deliberate, structured programme — not a parallel activity. The learning and development agenda should be as structured and measured as the commercial agenda.
Progress indicator
Development conversations with direct reports are regular, structured, documented. Second chair deployed as a team-wide methodology.
